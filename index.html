October 2019 – May 2021

SAN JACINTO COLLEGE PHYSICS DEPARTMENT · COORDINATOR: RADIA REDJIMI

My study was about the general-purpose detector, Compact Muon Solenoid, which detects and measures muons. Muons are particles much heavier than electrons and are created by the collision of cosmic rays. This project focused on studying the electronics used to detect these particles and the properties used to identify them. I analyzed the principle, implementation, and performance of a muon identification at the cathode strip chamber sub-detector. We work on building the school’s very first particle detector for the cosmic ray observatory.
